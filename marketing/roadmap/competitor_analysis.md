# ChairTime Competitor Analysis

## Executive Summary

ChairTime competes in the salon/spa booking software space, targeting hair salons, nail bars, barber shops, beauty therapists, and spas in Ireland and the UK. Competitors range from global players to niche beauty-focused tools.

**Our positioning**: Beautiful, simple booking software that salon owners actually enjoy using.

---

## Competitor Overview

### 1. Fresha (formerly Shedul)

**Overview**: Global leader in salon software. Originally free (Shedul), now freemium with payment processing fees.

**Pricing**:
- Core booking: Free
- Revenue: 2.19% + 20p per transaction (payment processing)
- Optional paid add-ons: Marketing, advanced features

**Strengths**:
- **Free core product** - hard to compete on price
- Excellent mobile app
- Marketplace for client discovery
- Comprehensive feature set
- Strong brand recognition

**Weaknesses**:
- **Hidden costs** - Payment processing adds up fast
- **Lock-in** - Must use their payments to get value
- **Support quality** declining as they scale
- **Generic** - not Irish/UK focused
- Overwhelming for small/solo salons

**Our Differentiation**:
- Transparent flat-rate pricing (no % fees)
- Use any payment processor you want
- Irish/UK focused support and features
- Simpler interface for smaller salons

---

### 2. Phorest

**Overview**: Irish-founded, now global. Premium positioning, feature-rich, targets growing salons.

**Pricing**:
- Starting ~€100/month
- Custom pricing based on salon size
- Setup fees common

**Strengths**:
- **Irish company** - understands local market
- Comprehensive feature set
- Strong marketing tools
- Excellent reporting
- Good training/support

**Weaknesses**:
- **Expensive** - overkill for small salons
- **Long contracts** often required
- **Complex** - steep learning curve
- Setup fees and hidden costs
- Sales-heavy approach

**Our Differentiation**:
- 5x cheaper for small salons
- No contracts, cancel anytime
- Simpler to use (setup in minutes, not days)
- No pushy sales process

---

### 3. GlossGenius

**Overview**: US-based, modern design, targeting independent beauty professionals. Growing UK presence.

**Pricing**:
- Standard: $24/month
- Gold: $48/month
- Payment processing: 2.6% + $0.15

**Strengths**:
- **Beautiful design** - Instagram-worthy
- Great mobile app
- Built-in website builder
- Strong for independents
- Modern, fresh brand

**Weaknesses**:
- **US-focused** - currency, tax, terminology
- Payment processing fees on top
- Limited team features
- No UK/Irish payment integration
- Support timezone issues

**Our Differentiation**:
- Built for Irish/UK market
- Proper VAT handling
- Local payment integrations (Stripe, SumUp)
- Local support in your timezone

---

### 4. Square Appointments

**Overview**: Part of Square ecosystem. Free for individuals, paid for teams. Payment processing required.

**Pricing**:
- Free: 1 user
- Plus: $29/month/location
- Premium: $69/month/location
- Payment processing: 2.6% + 10¢

**Strengths**:
- **Free tier** for solopreneurs
- Integrated POS system
- Strong brand (Square)
- Hardware options (terminals, readers)
- Good basic features

**Weaknesses**:
- **Must use Square payments** for best experience
- Generic (not salon-specific)
- Limited salon-specific features
- US-centric
- Support can be slow

**Our Differentiation**:
- Salon-specific features (waitlist, rebooking prompts)
- Use any payment system
- Better fit for salon workflow
- More personal support

---

### 5. Timely

**Overview**: New Zealand-based, strong in UK/Australia. Clean interface, good for small teams.

**Pricing**:
- Build: £15/month
- Elevate: £25/month
- Innovate: £35/month
- Per staff pricing can add up

**Strengths**:
- Clean, modern interface
- Good calendar functionality
- Reasonable pricing
- Good for small teams
- Solid feature set

**Weaknesses**:
- **Per-staff pricing** gets expensive
- Limited marketing features
- Not as well-known
- Support in NZ timezone
- Fewer integrations

**Our Differentiation**:
- Flat pricing regardless of team size
- Irish/UK timezone support
- More aggressive feature development
- Simpler pricing model

## Market Opportunities

### 1. Price Transparency Gap
- Fresha/Square "free" but take 2-3% of every transaction
- €10,000/month in card payments = €200-300 in fees
- Salon owners don't realise until later
- Opportunity: Transparent flat-rate pricing

### 2. Small Salon Neglect
- Phorest targets €100k+ salons
- Fresha becoming complex
- Solo beauticians and small salons underserved
- Opportunity: Simple, affordable, scales with you

### 3. Irish Market Specifically
- 4,000+ hair salons in Ireland
- 3,000+ beauty/nail businesses
- Phorest is Irish but expensive
- No Irish-founded affordable option
- "Support local" sentiment

### 4. Integration Flexibility
- Most competitors lock you into their payments
- Salons want to use their existing card terminal
- Opportunity: Work with any payment system

### 5. WhatsApp Booking
- Most competitors use SMS only
- WhatsApp dominant in Ireland for messaging
- Opportunity: WhatsApp booking confirmations/reminders

## Threats to Monitor

1. **Fresha becoming truly free** (subsidised by marketplace)
2. **Instagram adding native booking** (they've hinted at this)
3. **Google expanding Reserve with Google**
4. **Phorest launching budget tier**
5. **New entrant with VC funding**

## Strategic Recommendations

### Short-term (0-6 months)
1. **Nail the basics** - Booking, reminders, no-show protection
2. **Build testimonials** - 10+ video testimonials from real salons
3. **SEO for "salon booking software Ireland"**
4. **Partner with beauty colleges** - students become salon owners

### Medium-term (6-18 months)
1. **WhatsApp integration** - bookings and reminders via WhatsApp
2. **Instagram direct booking** - book button on salon profiles
3. **Referral program** - salons know salons
4. **Trade show presence** - Salon International, etc.

### Long-term (18+ months)
1. **Marketplace potential** - let clients discover salons
2. **Vertical expansion** - tattoo, wellness, fitness
3. **AI features** - smart scheduling, demand prediction
4. **European expansion** - similar markets

## Competitive Battlecards

### vs Fresha
**When prospect mentions Fresha:**

"Fresha is 'free' but takes 2.19% + 20p of every card payment. If you do €8,000/month in cards, that's €175+ in fees. ChairTime is €29/month flat - use any card terminal you want, keep 100% of your takings."

### vs Phorest
**When prospect mentions Phorest:**

"Phorest is brilliant for large salons with big budgets. But if you're a smaller salon, you'll pay for features you don't need. ChairTime gives you the booking essentials at a fraction of the price, and you can always upgrade as you grow."

### vs Square
**When prospect mentions Square:**

"Square is great if you're already in their ecosystem. But if you want flexibility to use any card reader, and you want salon-specific features like waitlists and rebooking prompts, ChairTime is built specifically for salons."

### vs "I use a paper diary"
**When prospect uses paper:**

"Paper diaries work until they don't - a lost book, double bookings, clients who forget appointments. ChairTime sends automatic reminders (cuts no-shows by 60%), lets clients book 24/7, and costs less than one no-show per month."
