### 200 Words
ChairTime is booking software built specifically for the beauty industry. Founded in Dublin in 2024, the company was created to solve a simple problem: salon owners spend too much time on admin and not enough time on the work they love.

The ChairTime platform enables salons, spas, barbers, and independent beauty professionals to accept online bookings around the clock. Clients book themselves via website, Instagram, or Google - no phone calls, no WhatsApp back-and-forth. Automatic SMS and email reminders reduce no-shows by an average of 60%, and smart waitlist features fill cancellations automatically.

Unlike enterprise salon management systems that cost hundreds per month and take weeks to implement, ChairTime is designed for simplicity. Most salons are fully set up in 15 minutes, and pricing starts at €29/month with no hidden fees or payment processing charges.

The platform serves over 1,000 salons across Ireland and the United Kingdom, from single-chair home salons to multi-stylist high-street locations. ChairTime has facilitated hundreds of thousands of bookings and helped salons recover significant revenue that would otherwise be lost to no-shows and missed booking opportunities.

## FAQ for Press

**Q: How is ChairTime different from Fresha or Phorest?**

A: Fresha is "free" but takes 2-3% of every payment - that adds up to hundreds per month for busy salons. Phorest is comprehensive but expensive and complex. ChairTime is simple, transparent flat-rate pricing, and focused on doing booking brilliantly.

**Q: What's the business model?**

A: SaaS subscription from €29/month. We don't take payment processing fees - salons use their own card terminals and keep 100%.

**Q: Who's your typical customer?**

A: Single stylists to salons with 5-10 staff. Hair salons, nail bars, beauty therapists, barbers, spas. Anyone who takes appointments in the beauty industry.

**Q: What's next for ChairTime?**

A: Deeper integrations (Instagram direct booking, Google Reserve), AI-powered demand forecasting, and expansion to more European markets with similar salon cultures.
