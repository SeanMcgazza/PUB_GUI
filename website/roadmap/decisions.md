# ChairTime Copy & Strategy Decisions

## Positioning Decisions

### Why Lead with "No Commission"
- **Decision:** Make zero commission the primary differentiator
- **Rationale:** This is the most frustrating pain point with existing platforms. Fresha, Treatwell, etc. take significant cuts. Leading with "keep 100%" immediately resonates with anyone who's felt that pain.
- **Trade-off:** May attract price-sensitive users; need to demonstrate value beyond just being cheap.

### Why "Stylists" and "Hairdressers" Not Just "Salons"
- **Decision:** Target individual practitioners, not just salon owners
- **Rationale:** The independent/freelance stylist market is growing. These users make their own software decisions and feel commission pain most directly. Salon owners come next.
- **Trade-off:** May seem small-time; enterprise salon chains might dismiss it.

### Why Not Compete on Features
- **Decision:** Position as simple and focused, not feature-rich
- **Rationale:** Phorest, Zenoti, etc. win on features. We can't compete there. We win on simplicity, price, and respecting users' business independence.
- **Trade-off:** Feature-seekers will go elsewhere. That's okay.

## Competitor Positioning

### vs Fresha
- **Position:** "Same booking convenience, without paying for it"
- **Angle:** Fresha's "new client" fees and payment processing cuts add up. We take nothing.
- **Messaging:** Don't attack Fresha directly; let users make the comparison.

### vs Treatwell / Booksy
- **Position:** "Your booking page, not a marketplace"
- **Angle:** Marketplace platforms send you clients but own the relationship. ChairTime is *your* tool for *your* clients.

### vs Phorest / Zenoti
- **Position:** "Right-sized for independents"
- **Angle:** Enterprise tools are overkill and overpriced for solo/small. We're not trying to be them.

### vs Pen & Paper
- **Position:** "Modern, but just as simple"
- **Angle:** If paper works for you, great. When you're ready for online booking without complexity, we're here.

## SEO Strategy Rationale

- **Volume:** Moderate but highly relevant traffic.

### Commission-Related Keywords
- **Why:** "No commission booking system" and "Fresha alternative" capture users actively looking to switch.

## Copy Tone Decisions

### Warm and Peer-Level
- **Decision:** Write like a fellow beauty professional, not a corporate software company.
- **Rationale:** Trust comes from feeling understood. Stylists are often ignored by tech companies or talked down to.
- **Example:** "Your clients book. You keep 100%." not "Optimise your appointment scheduling workflow."

### Honest About Limitations
- **Decision:** Be clear about what we don't do (payments, inventory, payroll).
- **Rationale:** Builds trust. Better to lose a wrong-fit user than frustrate someone who expected more.

### Feature Restraint
- **Decision:** Don't oversell features. List what matters; skip the fluff.
- **Rationale:** Stylists are busy. They want to know if it works, not read marketing prose.

---

## Pricing Communication

### Lead with Free
- **Decision:** Free tier prominently featured; paid tier positioned as "nice to have" not mandatory.
- **Rationale:** Reduces signup friction. Let the product prove itself before asking for money.

### "Commission-Free Forever" Promise
- **Decision:** Make zero commission a permanent, prominent promise.
- **Rationale:** This is our core differentiator. It should be unambiguous and trustworthy.

### Transparent Paid Features
- **Decision:** Clearly show what free includes vs paid upgrades.
- **Rationale:** Nobody likes surprise paywalls. Honesty upfront builds long-term trust.

---

## What We're NOT Doing

- No "Get Started" without knowing what we do (always explain first)
- No marketplace features (we don't broker clients to stylists)
- No aggressive upselling inside the product
- No premium-only features that cripple free tier usability
- No "Book a demo" gates—self-serve signup only
- No celebrity endorsements or influencer marketing (yet)
